--- Zombie and Orphan Cleanup Notes ---
Q: How are orphans cleaned up? A: Orphans are automatically re-parented to PID 1 (init/systemd), which then waits for them and cleans them up when they finish.
Q: How are zombies cleaned up? A: Zombies are cleaned up when the parent process calls wait() to collect the child's exit status. If the parent dies, init/systemd adopts and cleans them up.
Q: Can you kill a zombie with 'kill -9'? Why or why not? A: No. A zombie is already dead - it has no running code or memory. kill -9 only works on running processes. The only way to remove a zombie is to kill its parent so init can collect it.
